The Parent Key: one spine for 10.96 million identities

By Dr. Scoop · SaSMaster Identity Desk · 6 min read · refreshed today

Every audience question starts with the same hidden problem: is this the same title? The Parent Key is how SaSMaster answers it — one resolved identity per title, episode, and person, stitched from a dozen disagreeing identifier systems.

The catalog the industry runs on is a patchwork. The same film carries one number in one registry, a different number in another, a third in a rights system — and none agree on whether the 4K re-release is "the same movie." Before you can measure an audience, price an ad, or clear a right, you have to decide what counts as one thing.

A claim is only as trustworthy as the identity beneath it. The Parent Key is that identity.

One key, many aliases

The Parent Key resolves 10,955,204 entities into a single spine: 903,060 movies, 370,729 series, 9,681,539 episodes, and 3,220,202 people. Around each key we link alternate identifiers — 33,003 cross-system bridges so far, the large majority anchored to a certified EIDR.

Why certification matters

Of the movies in the spine, 227,395 are EIDR-certified — roughly a quarter. Certification is what lets a claim travel: a certified title matches across audience, advertising, and rights without guesswork. The uncertified three-quarters is both a risk and an opportunity. Built clean-room, from first principles — the spine is the foundation every Audience Story stands on.
